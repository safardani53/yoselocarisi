Final day to claim between $300 to $600 from $17million pot - see if youre owed a robocall settle

TODAY is the last day Americans can claim up to $600 from the $17million DirecTV settlement.

The plaintiffs in the class action lawsuit, Brown, et al. v. DirecTV, are alleging that the company hired debt collectors to send recorded warnings of overdue payments.

The problem is that the robocalls were given to 220,000 folks who were not customers, a direct violation of the 1991 Telephone Consumer Protection Act.

The 1991 law states that express consent is required to send prerecorded messages over the phone.

According to the U.S. Federal Communications Commission (FCC), express content is permission given by someone on paper or electronically to receive marketing messages sent using an autodialer.

Lead plaintiffs Jenny Brown and Carmen Montijo filed the claim alleging that DirecTV hired several companies to issue at least 220,000 calls to wrong numbers.

The suit alleges that the company "either knew or consciously avoided knowing," what the telemarketing agencies were doing.

Are you eligible?

Anyone who has not been a DirecTV customer since October 2004 but still received the calls qualifies for the payments.

Additionally, the messages must have been sent between the dates August 14, 2009, and December 4, 2015.

The messages must have either been prerecorded or have an artificial voice.

Depending on which agency contacted you, the amount of money may vary from $300 up to $600.

However, the exact amount depends on how many people file successful claims.

If a consumer was called by iQor or Credit management, they may qualify for two shares or around $600 per call.

If they were contacted by Enhanced Recovery or AFNI, they can get one share or around $300 per call.

Filing a claim

To file a claim, visit the settlement website and correctly fill out a form.

Consumers may also mail a claim form to:

DIRECTV Prerecord TCPA Settlement Administrator
P.O. Box 25356
Richmond, VA 23260

You are not required to provide proof, just the cell numbers that received the call.

The deadline to file a claim is December 19 and the final hearing to approve the settlement is set for February 24.

Payments should start being sent out after that date.
