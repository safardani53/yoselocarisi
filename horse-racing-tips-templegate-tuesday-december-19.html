Horse racing tips: Templegate landed a 29-1 treble on Monday and Tuesday's NAP looks a natural for f

TEMPLEGATE banged in a brilliant 29-1 treble on Monday and goes hunting for Christmas funds on Tuesday.

Back a horse at the best price by clicking their odds below.

OUR NEL (12.40 Fakenham, Nap)

Sun Racing columnist Harry Fry sends this strapping mare over fences for the first time today and she can get off to a winning start. She has shown some promise over the smaller obstacles, including when second at Worcester and third at Market Rasen. But Fry has made the right call in sending her over fences after just three hurdles runs. She has plenty of scope to jump a fence and the challenge of the bigger obstacles can help with the keenness she has shown in her races.

SOLDIER OF DESTINY (2.00 Catterick, nb)

Jamie Snowden has been in excellent form all season and this seven-year-old signed off his last campaign with a close second at Huntingdon. Despite being an experienced horse — with two chase wins to his name — he is allowed to run in the novice hurdle ranks. He has shown his best form over this distance and has performed on more undulating tracks too so should be fine with Catterick’s rollercoaster ride. Anything close to his best form would be good enough to win this.

BRING THE ACTION (12.20 Wincanton, treble)

Ran his best race for a long time when second at Doncaster 17 days ago. He appreciated the test of stamina on Town Moor and that staying power will be an asset at tough Wincanton. Nigel Hawke’s gelding continues to come down the weights and is now 7lb lower than his last win. Regular pilot Tom Buckley can ride him in this conditionals’ race and he should go close again.
