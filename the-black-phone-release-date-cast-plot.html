The Black Phone: release date, cast, and plot

BASED on Joe Hill's 2004 short story of the same name, The Black Phone is a supernatural horror movie premiering in 2022.

Here is everything we know about the upcoming film.

When is The Black Phone's release date?

Initially set to release in February of 2022, Universal Pictures pushed the premiere date of The Black Phone to summer.

Now expected on June 24, 2022, The Black Phone is a horror film from the director of Doctor Strange, Scott Derrickson.

Who is in the cast of The Black Phone?

Ethan Hawke as The Grabber

The Grabber is a serial killer who preys on young children in a suburban Colorado town.

He is portrayed by Ethan Hawke, an actor and filmmaker known for his work in film and theatre.

Mason Thames as Finney Shaw

Finney Shaw is one of The Grabber's latest victims, who can speak to the serial killer's previous fatalities through a disconnected phone.

Mason Thames made his on-screen debut in 2017 with the short film, After Omelas.

Madeleine McGraw as Gwen Shaw

Gwen Shaw is Finney's sister who uses her psychic dreams to try and locate her stolen brother.

Madeleine McGraw first appeared as an extra on Bones in 2014 and has since starred in several films and television shows.

Jeremy Davies as Mr Shaw

Seasoned actor Jeremy Davies portrays the father of Finney and Gwen, Mr Shaw.

Davies is an award-winning actor known for his roles in Lost (2008-10), Justified (2011-15), and Saving Private Ryan (1998).

What is The Black Phone about?

Based on a short story from 2004, The Black Phone follows Finney Shaw as he attempts to escape from the sadistic serial killer, The Grabber.

After five children go missing in a 1978 Colorado town, Finney Shaw is thrown into the back of a van and knocked unconscious.

He wakes in a soundproof basement, where a disconnected phone leads him to hear the voices of The Grabber's previous victims - all of which are dead.

Finney's sister begins to have dreams about her brother and sets out to find him.
