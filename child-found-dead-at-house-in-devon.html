Child found dead at house in Devon seaside resort after cops called The Sun

COPS are probing after a child was found dead in a seaside town.

Emergency services descended on the property in Arcot Park in Sidmouth, east Devon, after a call reporting concern for a child.

Medics battled to save the youngster - as yet unidentified - but they were was declared dead at the scene.

Police called it a "desperately tragic" incident and said officers are supporting the family.

An investigation has now been launched although the death is not being treated as suspicious.

A spokesman for Devon and Cornwall Police said: “Police and Ambulance were called to Arcot Park in Sidmouth, at 5pm on Tuesday October 15, after a concern was raised for the welfare of a child within a property.

“Emergency services attended the location and despite their best efforts, the child was declared deceased at the scene.

“This is a desperately sad and tragic incident for everyone involved and our thoughts are with the child’s family and friends at this incredibly difficult time.

“The death is not being treated as suspicious and police are continuing their enquiries on behalf of the coroner.”
