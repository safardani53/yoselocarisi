Howard Leslie Shore is a Canadian composer and conductor known for his cinematic work. He has composed music for more than 80 films, including scores for trilogy The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit. He won three Academy Awards for The Lord of the Rings, including one for "Into the West", an award he shared with Eurythmics lead singer Annie Lennox and writer. / producer Fran Walsh, who wrote the lyrics. He is also a frequent collaborator with director David Cronenberg, having recorded all but one of his films since 1979. Shore has also composed a number of concert works including an opera, The Fly, based on the plot of Cronenberg's film. from 1986, premiered at the Théâtre du Châtelet in Paris on July 2, 2008; a short film titled Fanfare for Wanamaker Organ and Philadelphia Orchestra; and a brief overture for the Swiss Symphony Orchestra of the 21st Century.
