Dale Winton was left broken after break up with boyfriend who he would commute to Florida to be with

TRAGIC Dale Winton “went off the radar” after breaking up with a boyfriend who he moved to Florida to be with, a friend has said.

The TV host, who was found dead at his home on Wednesday at the age of 62, spoke publicly about his struggles with depression and his deep desire to find love.

As his television career stalled, the star was forced to sell his beloved Regent’s Park mansion, and according to friends, had become increasingly erratic and withdrawn.

And now a celeb pal of Dale’s has revealed that the Supermarket Sweep presenter had been lonely in recent months following the break-up of a relationship.

Dale was so devoted to the mystery man that he would undertake a regular transatlantic commute in between jobs in Britain.

They said: “I know that he took himself off to Florida to live with this guy and that relationship went south and that triggered all of this.

David Walliams reveals he was worried about close friend Dale Winton before his death

“Dale had been devoted – he would come over to Britain, do his filming and then head straight back to Florida again.

“They were happy, but then that was over.

“After that, he went off the radar. It was really difficult to get hold of him or be anywhere near him.

“If he had been lucky enough to have a relationship with a man who loved him, then I think he would be alive today.”

The TV host added the pressures to stay young to his unhappiness when he admitted to taking “super strong slimming pills and amphetamines” in a bid to stay in shape.

His openness of his plight to stay youthful and look good lists two face lifts and a nose job, but it transcended into the star becoming a 'yo-yo dieter'.

Dale Winton’s heartbreaking last interview in which he opened up about mental health and joked about terrible taste in men

In a TV interview two years ago, Dale admitted that his struggle with depression was triggered by a “bad break up” and at its worst he found it difficult to leave his house.

The star said: “Nobody wants a camp, over 60-year-old man.

"I can't go on dating sites."

Dale’s best pal, radio DJ Steve Allen, fought back tears yesterday as he revealed the presenter had recently admitted he’d “had enough".

He added: “I feel better for him now, because I don’t think he’d been in a good place for a long, long time.

"I feel happy for him that he’s in a place now where he’s probably going to be eternally young.”

In the years leading up to Dale’s death, the presenter had briefly returned to screens in Florida Fly Drive on Channel 5, but just one episode of the show was shown before it was pulled from air.

Steve went on: “He was just getting deeper and deeper into debt because unfortunately the one thing Dale loved doing was spending money.

“He liked to be seen spending money, he liked going out spending money. He didn’t want people thinking he didn’t have any money.”

The Supermarket Sweep icon's best friend also revealed he had a secret fling with a "very famous actor" who was not openly gay.

Police last night said the star’s death at his new home was “non-suspicious” and officers will submit a file to the coroner.

Dale’s comedian pal David Walliams told how “he was one of the funniest people I ever met”.

He said: “I think of him as a cross between a teddy bear and an angel. He was so wonderful at being famous. He would give people so much of his time.”

He added: “He just made people happy when he came on screen. I just wish he could find happiness himself.”

Dale Winton had plenty of catch phrases on Supermarket Sweep

CCTV has been released of the late star looking happy, 'laughing and joking with friends' in a London restaurant.

A waiter for the restaurant said Dale was a frequent visitor and looked in good health during his final days: "He was a nice guy, very down-to-earth and polite."

Dale’s early childhood was blighted by tragedy after losing both his parents by the age of 21.

His mum Sheree, a glamourous actress who had once appeared in a Bond film, took her own life age 39.

Dale found her dead after she put a ‘Do Not Disturb’ sign on her bedroom door.

He later revealed his deep regret at never telling her the truth about his sexuality, instead coming out as gay when he was 47.
