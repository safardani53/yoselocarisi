Judge slams cyclist Charlie Alliston who killed mum-of-two Kim Briggs while riding banned bike for '

A JUDGE has blasted a cyclist who killed a mum-of-two while riding a banned racing bike for "thrills".

Charlie Alliston, 20, crashed into HR executive Kim Briggs, 44, as she tried to cross a busy road in East London.

As Kim lay dying in hospital from severe head injuries, Alliston blamed her for the crash in a series of online posts.

Following a trial at the Old Bailey, he was cleared of manslaughter but found guilty of causing bodily harm by wanton and furious driving.

Today a judge branded Alliston an "accident waiting to happen" as she sentenced him to 18 months behind bars.

Judge Wendy Joseph QC said: "I am satisfied in some part it was this so-called thrill that motivated you to ride without a front brake shouting and swearing at pedestrians to get out of the way.

"I've heard your evidence and I have no doubt that even now you remain obstinately sure of yourself and your own abilities.

"I have no doubt you are wrong in this.

"The victim could have been any pedestrian.

"It was in fact Mrs Kim Briggs."

The trial heard Alliston crashed into Kim while riding his Â£470 Planet X carbon frame bike at up to 14mph.

After the crash the former bicycle courier, then 18, took to social media to describe how he twice warned pedestrian Kim to "get the f*** outta my way" in Shoreditch, East London.

He wrote: "We collided pretty hard, our heads hit together, hers went into the floor and ricocheted into mine.

"It is a pretty serious incident so I won't bother saying, 'oh she deserved it, it's her fault'.

"Yes it is her fault but no, she did not deserve it.

"Hopefully, it is a lesson learned on her behalf, it shouldn't have happened like it did but what more can I say."

He added: "It's not my fault people either think they are invincible or have zero respect for cyclists."

Prosecutors took the unprecedented step of bringing a manslaughter charge due to the unusual circumstances of the case.

Jurors took more than 12 hours to clear Alliston of manslaughter but convict him of a lesser offence of causing bodily harm by wanton and furious driving.

Kim's husband Matt has since called for a new law of causing death by dangerous cycling similar to that faced by motorists.

Today he urged bike sellers and courier companies to get "lethal" bikes with no front brakes off the road.

Matt said in a statement outside court: "This case has clearly and evidently demonstrated there is a gap in the law when it comes to death and serious injury by dangerous cycling.

"To have to rely on either manslaughter at one end or a Victorian law that doesn't even mention causing death at the other end tells you that there is a gap.

"The fact that what happened to Kim is rare is not a reason to be no remedy.

"I would like to call on courier companies and bike retailers to get fixed wheel bikes without brakes off the road.

"They are illegal and as we have seen with Kim's death, they are potentially lethal."

Alliston's mum Karen expressed her "sincere condolences" to the family of his victim after he was jailed.

She said: "We know that they bear the heaviest loss of all in this case.

"My son Charlie whilst acquitted of the most serious charge in this case has been sentenced appropriately."
