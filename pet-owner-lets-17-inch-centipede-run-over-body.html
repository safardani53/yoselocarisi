Brave pet owner lets his beloved 17-inch venomous centipede run all over his body

A “CRAZY” Taiwanese man lets his giant venomous centipede crawl all over his body – and even STROKES the critter’s head.

More bizarrely, Neil Cheng Le braves the poisonous fangs to hand-feed his 17-inch-long pet her favourite treat, cat food.

But the biology student, aged 26, has warned his exotic choice of pet is not for everyone.

He bought the yellow-legged female Peruvian giant centipede in 2014 when she was just a fraction of her fully-grown size.

Also known as an Amazonian giant centipede (Scolopendra gigantean), Neil said: “I have no problem letting her crawl over me and hand feeding her.”

Unsettling footage from last week shows him letting the nameless critter scamper over his body before feasting on cat food.

The video shows the centipede emerging from her plastic home, and run up his right arm.

In a particularly creepy-crawly moment she scuttles towards his face.

But Neil doesn’t flinch, despite having her venom-bearing fangs within striking distance.

He is also shown giving his unusual pet cat food, which she eats from his hand, before allowing him to stroke her head.

But the student doesn’t recommend his unique companion for everyone and warned that only experts should handle such dangerous creatures.

Neil said: “There’s definitely a risk handling venomous [arthropods] and it should be left to those with experience.

“Their bite can definitely be painful but I never feel unsafe as I’m an experienced arthropod hobbyist.

“I have no problem letting her crawl over me and hand-feeding her.”

Centipede bites, which are inflicted through their pincer-like front claws, can result in agonising pain, a blistering rash, nausea and fever.

Their bites are so venomous – they can subdue prey 15 times their size – that they can subdue mice, and even bats substantially heavier than themselves.

In 2000, a researcher wrote about a newborn being envenomed in Venezuela, after she was bitten by the same type of centipede.

The 28-day-old baby girl – who survived the bite – suffered intense pain, cried uncontrollably, and suffered from blood clots where her skin was punctured.

Her pulse rate and temperature soared, and her hand and arm were swollen for three days.

Despite such traits, Neil is clearly fond of the carnivorous arthropod, saying: “She has always been very gentle and easy to handle.

“However you need the right experience and knowledge to keep them healthy.”

It’s small wonder that the student has to pick his roommates carefully, however.

He said: “My roommate is just as crazy as me – he has his own venomous pets.”
