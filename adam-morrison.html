Adam Morrison: Career

Adam’s career officially started from his high school days, but it took a new turn, and he reached a different level of success from his University career.

Again in year two, he made Gonzaga win WCC; with Adam’s arrival, Gonzaga reached its new height. 

In the 2005-06 season, Adam couldn’t perform well in the initial phase, but as an actual player, he bounced back, and after two losses, he again won the third match and reached his career-high points, i.e., 44 points.

2006 was a challenging year for Morrison; he burst into tears during a match and collapsed. For this, many criticized Adam, but many came to his support.

Professional Career

In 2006 Adam was drafted by the Charlotte Bobcats; his professional journey started. Besides, he signed a contract for two years, and his debut match was with the Indian Padres, where he scored 14 points.

Besides, Adam was the first player drafted by Michael Jordan in his tenure as Manager of Basketball Operations at the Bobcats.

Adam didn’t let Jordan down and proved his worth by scoring 30 points, his career-highest point then.

Everything was going fine, but in 2007 Adam couldn’t perform due to his knee injury and had to rest after the surgery. In the healing process, he missed the whole 2007-08 season.

After that, Adam’s contract with Bobcats ended, and after recovery, Adam got drafted by Los Angeles Lakers.

Even though Adam won NBA championships with the Lakers in 2009 and 2010, he only got limited playtime, and after that, he got released from there the same year.
