SALMA Hayek has been married for more than a decade.

In a February, 2021, interview, the actress said that living with François-Henri Pinault has made her "become a much better person."

Who is François-Henri Pinault?

Francois-Henri Pinault is a billionaire businessman from France who has been married to actress Salma Hayek since February, 2009.

The pair tied the knot on Valentine's Day in Paris, two years after announcing their engagement in 2007.

They renewed their wedding vows on April 25, 2009.

Pinault is honorary chairman of the luxury goods company Kering - founded by his father, François Pinault - which owns fashion brands Saint Laurent, Alexander McQueen and Gucci.

He is also the president of Groupe Artémis since 2003, a company with similar investments in fashion, food, wine, luxury, art and tourism.

What is François-Henri Pinault's net worth?

Much like his father, whose net worth is estimated at $49.4billion, François-Henri has made himself a fortune.

What has Salma Hayek said about her husband?

Set to make an appearance on The Graham Norton Show on Friday, October 29, 2021, Salma will undoubtedly have a few words to say about the man sharing her life.

The 55-year-old actress already spoke extensively about her marriage while appearing with Dax Shepard on his Armchair Expert podcast.

She said: "In pictures you cannot begin to guess the magic in him.

"He's made me become a much better person and grow in such a good, healthy way.

"When I married him, everybody said, 'Oh, it's an arranged marriage. She married him for the money.'

"I'm like, 'Yeah, whatever, b**ch. Think what you want.

"Fifteen years together. And we are strong in love, and I don't even get offended." 

She also opened up about her husband's hectic schedule and how they balance family time.

She shared: "My guy finishes work, no matter how hard it was, and trust me, he has a lot of responsibilities, big smile on his face, happy to be home, happy to see me and the kids, make us laugh.

"We go on vacation, he completely shuts off.

"He's in the moment.

"It's not just an insult to me. I'm not the one being judged only.

"They cannot begin to imagine what a joy that human being is."
