Jeff Cook dead at 73: Alabama co-founder and Country Music Hall of Famer dies after Parkinsons di

JEFF Cook, one of the founding members of the country music band Alabama, has died at 73.

Cook died on Monday in his home in Destin, Florida, following a years-long battle with Parkinson's disease, a representative for the band confirmed.

The legendary guitarist battled Parkinson's disease for nearly a decade, publically disclosing his diagnosis in 2017.

He told News 19 at the time: "I’m not calling it quits, but sometimes our bodies dictate what we have to do, and mine is telling me it’s time to take a break and heal.

"I do believe in prayer and I appreciate all the continued prayers throughout this difficult time.”

Parkinson's disease is a progressive disorder that affects the nervous system and the parts of the body controlled by the nerves, according to the Mayo Clinic.

Cook founded the band along with his cousins Randy Owen and Teddy Gentry in 1972.

The trio, who first went by the moniker Wildcountry, changed their name to Alabama in 1977.

The band changed the landscape of country music with their blend of traditional country style and southern rock mix, selling more than 80million albums with over 40 No.1 hit.

Alabama landed eight No.1 songs on the country charts between the spring of 1980 and the summer of 1982.

They were responsible for a slew of hits, including Mountain Music, I'm In A Hurry, Cheap Seats and My Home Is Alabama.

The group was inducted into the Musicians Hall of Fame and Museum in 2019 and was awarded the institution's first-ever Life Time Achievement Award.

Cook is survived by his wife, Lisa Cook, his mother, Betty Cook, brother David Cook, father-in-law Jerrial Williams, and nieces and nephews.
