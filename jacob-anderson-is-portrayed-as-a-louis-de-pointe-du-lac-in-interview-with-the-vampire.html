Jacob Anderson Is Portrayed As A Louis de Pointe du Lac In Interview With the Vampire

Jacob Anderson, well known for his role as Grey Worm in the Game of Thrones series, will now portray Louis de Pointe du Lac in AMC’s new drama Interview With the Vampire. On Sunday, October 2, 2022, the program will debut on AMC and AMC+. Based on Anne Rice’s novel of the same name, Interview With the Vampire chronicles the epic saga of blood, passion, and the hazards of immortality as related to fictitious journalist Daniel Molloy by Louis de Pointe du Lac, Lestat de Lioncourt, and Claudia. Continue reading to find out more about Jacob Anderson and his character Louis de Pointe du Lac.

Grey Worm was played by Jacob Anderson in the Game of Thrones series.

Jacob Anderson is an English actor and musician best known for his portrayal of Grey Worm in the Emmy-winning television series Game of Thrones. Grey Worm commanded Daenerys Targaryen’s Unsullied Army. Anderson was on the show from season 3 through season 8. He was elevated to the main cast in the finale. Anderson has starred in episodes such as ITV’s Injustice, Episodes, The Mimic, and Broadchurch in addition to Game of Thrones. When it was revealed that he will be joining Season 13 of Doctor Who in 2021, the actor became the talk of the town.

Adulthood, Chatroom, 4.3.2.1., Demons Never Die, Comedown, Offender, and Overlord are among Jacob Anderson’s cinematic credits. He also has a successful singing career, having released songs such as Stronger Than Ever, You’re a Man Now, Boy, Don’t Cry For Me, Aristocrats, and Say What You Mean.

Anderson is expected to play Louis de Pointe du Lac in the film adaptation of Anne Rice’s book Interview With the Vampire. It all starts with a black man’s restrictions in 1910 New Orleans, and the subsequent alluring invitation of Lestat de Lioncourt to join him as his vampire companion.

Anderson revealed to Collider about his character:

“I appreciate how much Louis suffers throughout the novel. I like how he is lamenting his previous life, and our Louis is very much the same. But I’d say our Louis packs a little more punch. I believe he is more likely to attack Lestat. He’s a little less moody at first, but we get there! We get there, but I think he’s got a little more bite.”

Anderson’s character is shown chatting to reporter Daniel Molloy about Lestat de Lioncourt in the Interview With the Vampire trailer, saying, “He is my killer, my mentor, my lover, and my creator.”

More details about Interview With the Vampire

Interview With the Vampire follows vampire Louis de Pointe du Lac as he tells a reporter about his existence. The material was previously turned into the 1994 film of the same name, starring Brad Pitt and Tom Cruise. Along with Jacob Anderson, actors Sam Reid as Lestat de Lioncourt, Bailey Bass as Claudia, and Eric Bogosian as Daniel Molloy will appear in Interview With the Vampire.
