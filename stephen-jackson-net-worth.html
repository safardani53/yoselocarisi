Stephen Jackson Net Worth: Career [2023 Update]

Stephen Jackson, originally Stephen Jess Jackson, is an American former basketball player who played in the NBA for 14 seasons and won the championship in 2003 as part of the San Antonio Spurs. Over the course of his 14 seasons in the league, he earned more than $68.6 million in salary alone. As of December 2023, Stephen Jackson has a net worth is estimated to be around $20 million.

Even though the shooting guard bounced from team to team, he garnered unparalleled success in the league setting multiple records in the process.

After retiring from the NBA in 2015, Jackson joined the BIG3 basketball league with the Killer 3s playing alongside Chauncey Billups.

In 2021, he became the head coach of the BIG3 team Trilogy and led the team to back-to-back BIG3 Championships.

Stephen Jackson | Early Life & Education

Stephen Jackson was born on April 5, 1978, in Houston, Texas, to his father, George Aldridge, and mother, Judyette Jackson.

He was raised by Judyette, a single mother in Port Arthur, Texas.

Despite working two jobs, his mother struggled to put the food on the table, and that was when Jackson began working in his grandfather’s soul food restaurant in Port Arthur. He used to bus tables and wash dishes there.

Coming to the education part, Jackson went to Lincoln High School and played basketball for the Lincoln Tigers’ men’s basketball team.

In his junior year, he led the team to a state championship.

Nevertheless, because of academic ineligibility at Lincoln, he transferred to Oka Hill Academy in Virginia.

Even though he was an exceptional basketball player, his academic was not that great. He had initially committed to joining the University of Arizona but was ruled ineligible due to low ACT and SAT scores.

Jackson then went to Butler County Community College in El Dorado, Kansas but did not play basketball there.

Stephen Jackson | Professional Career

Jackson was drafted by the Phoenix Suns 42nd overall in the 1997 NBA Draft. However, Suns waived Jackson on October 30.

He then went on to play in the Continental Basketball Association (CBA) for La Crosse Bobcats over two on-and-off seasons.

While with the Bobcats, he averaged 2.7 points in 12.7 minutes per game.

Similarly, in 1998 he played four games for the Sydney Kings in NBA Australia.

To hone his skills and advance in basketball, he further played professionally in the Dominican Republic and Venezuela.

Despite being drafted in the late 90s in the NBA, Stephen did not play until the 2000-01 season in the league.

Then 2001 season saw him play with the New Jersey Nets.

Even though he played 77 games with 40 starts, the Nets did not retain him, and that was when the San Antonio Spurs signed him.

Jackson helped the Spurs win their second NBA title during the team’s run in the 2003 NBA Playoffs.

He averaged 12.8 points per game, making him the team’s third-leading scorer.

The same year he rejected the Spur’s contract extension and signed a two-year deal with the Hawks.

After the 2003-04 season, he was traded to the Pacers.

On January 2007, the Pacers traded Jackson to the Golden State Warriors.

He additionally played for Charlotte Bobcats and Milwaukee Bucks and returned to Spurs for a brief and finally retired following the 2013-2014 season with the Clippers.

Jackson announced his retirement on July 22, 2015. After NBA, he tried his hands with the BIG3 league and then podcasting.

First Pay Check

We all are familiar with Shaquille O’Neal’s story. He spent his signing bonus of $1 million in a matter of hours. Even though he has climbed through the situation, some people are just less fortunate.

In the case of Jackson, when he was signed by the Phoenix Suns in 1997, he received $25,000, his first-ever paycheck.

Jackson hails from a low-income family raised by a single mother working multiple jobs to put food on the table.

When he received $25 grand, he, along with his friend Mike Bibby went to the mall and bought everything they could put their hands into.

When they got back home, Jackson just had $4-$5 grand in his pocket.

This was an eye-opener for Jackson. He recently revealed that the majority of his decision-making came from never being exposed to such money and the right financial advisors.

 Luckily for Stephen, his financial decision-making has drastically improved over time. Otherwise, he would have squandered his $68.6 million career earnings in a spending spree.

Contracts

In 2000, Jackson signed a $316,969 one-year deal with the New Jersey Nets.

And after a few low-value contract extensions, he hit the jackpot with Indiana Pacers. On July 15, 2004, Jackson signed a six-year $38.25 million contract with the team as part of a sign-and-trade.

This was his largest career contract and came with an average annual salary of $6.37 million.

Similarly, when he was traded to the Warriors, he signed a three-year $28 million contract with the team on November 17, 2008.

According to the contract, he received an average annual salary of $9.25 million.

All in all, Jackson made $68.6 million from NBA salary with different teams over 14 seasons.

Stephen Jackson | Legal Issues & Controversies

When you are rich and famous, controversies follow you everywhere.

Stephen has had his share of controversy and legal issues.

In 2004 and 2006, the shooting guard pled guilty to felony criminal recklessness.

He was charged with assault, two counts of battery, and disorderly conduct.

He received a lot of criticism for his legendary NBA players, such as Charles Barkley and Kareem Abdul-Jabbar.
