Career

Klein worked on Howard Dean’s Vermont primary campaign in 2003 and interned for the Washington Monthly in Washington, D.C. in 2004. He and Moulitsas were two of the first bloggers to report from a political meeting of the California State Democratic Party in 2003. On December 10, 2007, Klein moved his blog full-time to the American Prospect.

Klein joined Bloomberg View as a writer in May of 2011, in addition to his roles at The Washington Post and MSNBC. Klein announced in January 2014 that he would be leaving The Washington Post to launch a new media company with several other veteran journalists. Klein had initially “proposed the formation of an independent, explanatory journalism website—comprising more than three dozen staffers” with a yearly budget of more than US$10 million in order to avoid leaving The Washington Post.

Klein was the editor-in-chief of Vox before becoming editor-at-large, and she previously wrote for and edited The Washington Post’s Wonkblog. On MSNBC’s The Last Word with Lawrence O’Donnell, he frequently provides political reporting. Hardball with Chris Matthews and Rachel Maddow. He previously worked as a Countdown analyst alongside Keith Olbermann. On 14 March 2013, The Week magazine announced that Klein was one of several people being considered to host MSNBC’s yet-unknown 8 p.m. weekday prime-time segment that would replace The Ed Show.

In October of 2015, Klein, along with Matt Yglesias and Sarah Kliff, launched The Weeds, a Vox podcast of in-depth discussions about public policy. Klein is also the host of the podcast The Ezra Klein Show. Klein is the executive producer of Vox’s Netflix series Explained, which debuted in 2018. Klein, along with some Vox Media reporters, launched the Impeachment, Explained podcast in October of 2019.

Ezra Klein Achievements and Awards

He was named Blogger of the Year in 2010 by The Week magazine and The Sidney Hillman Foundation. GQ named him one of the 50 most powerful people in Washington, D.C. in 2011. In 2011, Time magazine named his blog one of the 25 best financial blogs.
