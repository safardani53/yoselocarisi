Cesare Borgia (Religious Leader) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Cesare Borgia is an Italian politician whose power struggle is the main inspiration for Prince Machiavelli. He is the bastard son of Pope Alexander VI and a member of the Hispanic-Aragonese Borgia family. After entering the church and becoming a cardinal in his father's election to the papacy, following the death of his brother in 1498, he became the first person to resign as cardinal. He served as center-back for King Louis XII of France around 1500, and occupied Milan and Naples during wars in Italy. At the same time, he established a state in central Italy, but after the death of his father he was unable to remain in power for a long time. According to Machiavelli, this was not due to lack of anticipation, but his mistake in creating a new pope.
